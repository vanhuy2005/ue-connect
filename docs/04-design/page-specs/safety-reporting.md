# safety-reporting.md

## 1. Purpose
Describe reporting, blocking, and safety escalation flows.

## 2. Design Decision
Use direct, reassuring, and non-judgmental safety language.

## 3. Rationale
Safety flows need to reduce friction while still making consequences clear.

## 4. Rules
- Show report and block options clearly.
- Explain the outcome of each action.
- Avoid blaming or shaming language.

## 5. Do / Don't
Do: make safety actions simple and calm.

Don't: bury reporting behind multiple unclear steps.

## 6. Tokens / Specs
Use caution colors only where a real risk or warning exists, with strong contrast and readable copy.

## 7. Component / Screen Impact
Impacts report sheets, confirmation states, moderation notices, and blocked-user feedback.

## 8. QA Checklist
- Safety actions are easy to find.
- Confirmation text is clear.
- The flow does not feel accusatory.

## 9. AI Prompt Notes
Ask for a safety workflow that feels trustworthy, calm, and direct.
